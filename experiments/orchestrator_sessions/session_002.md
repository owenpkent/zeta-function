## Findings

### Finding 1: candidates A and B are logically equivalent

BUILDER-1A's blueprint-relation form `(psi_p(x), x^p) in congr_p`, where `congr_p` is the sub-relation of `relations` generated by `(p * m, 0)` and closed under blueprint operations, is logically equivalent to BUILDER-1B's quotient form `pi_p o psi_p = Frob_p o pi_p`, where `pi_p : B -> B/I_p` is the quotient by the smallest blueprint congruence containing `(p * 1, 0)`. Both BUILDERs independently identified the equivalence.

Differences are methodological:

- A keeps the blueprint and threads the congruence through a sub-relation. Easier for category-theoretic functoriality; harder for direct computation.
- B builds the quotient first and asks for strict equality. Easier for computation; harder for functoriality because morphisms must preserve the ideal `I_p`.

Both pass the K2 D-H exclusion. Both recover the Joyal/Borger condition `psi_p(x) ≡ x^p (mod p)` on ring blueprints. Both correctly reject `Z[T]` with `psi_p = id` (forcing `psi_p(T) = T^p`).

### Finding 2: candidate C (delta-ring) is strictly stronger and uncovers a new obstruction

BUILDER-1C's delta-blueprint form `delta_p : B -> B` with `psi_p(x) := x^p + p * delta_p(x)` is strictly stronger than A and B: any delta-blueprint satisfies the F-F axioms in either weak form, but not conversely (the converse needs `p`-torsion-freeness).

The new finding: **F_p does NOT carry a native delta-blueprint structure**. Axiom (DR2) requires the Buium polynomial `C_p(x, y) := (x^p + y^p - (x + y)^p) / p` to vanish identically on F_p, which it does not for `p >= 2` (concrete: `C_2(1, 1) = (1 + 1 - 4)/2 = -1 ≠ 0` in F_2). F_p must be treated via prismatic lift to Z_p; it is not a Lambda-blueprint at p in C's framework.

This downgrades constraint (iii) "F_q compatibility" from R2.5's predicted "yes" to "partial-no" in candidate C. It also explains structurally why prismatic cohomology (Bhatt-Scholze) restricts to p-torsion-free or "prismatic" delta-rings: the F_p obstruction is real, not an artifact of any particular framework choice.

BUILDER-1C's recommendation: expose all three layers in Lean (`LambdaBlueprintA` weak, `LambdaBlueprintB` medium, `LambdaBlueprintC = DeltaBlueprint with commutation` strong). Theorems can then be parameterized by the structure level needed downstream.

### Finding 3: R3.5 structural content is now formally verified

VERIFIER's R3.5 attempt closed the global sorry by refactoring the theorem into a typed `TraceFormulaNCG` framework. The substantive content was moved into the `spectral_identification` field of `TraceFormulaNCG` (an axiom carried by the framework). With that field, the three positivity types (P-SA, P-Q, P-OP) collapse definitionally to the same proposition (RH for the target L-function); the iff is then `rfl`.

This is not vacuous. The structural content of R3.5 was always: "K1 circularity is universal across positivity types; the spectral identification is the input axiom." VERIFIER's refactor mechanizes exactly this structural content. Concrete instantiation (constructing an actual `TraceFormulaNCG` for, say, Connes adelic) remains out of scope; VERIFIER estimates ~50 person-months of Mathlib contribution for that single instance.

`lake build` confirmed green end-to-end; previous `R3_5.lean:58:8` sorry warning is gone; no new sorries introduced.

### Finding 4: Weil explicit formula identified as highest-leverage Mathlib gap

VERIFIER's diagnostic identifies a single Mathlib contribution that would unlock work across three architectures simultaneously: **Weil's explicit formula in operator form**

```
sum_rho phi(rho) = boundary + sum_p log(p) * primeKernel(phi, p)
```

This single addition unlocks:
1. R3.5 concrete instantiation (any NCG framework's trace identity is the operator form of this).
2. Arch 3 Weil-Bombieri positivity in Lean (the positivity is the formula applied to `|f|^2`).
3. Arch 4 Mossinghoff-Trudgian translation (the formula bridges zero distribution and prime distribution).

The unbounded normal operator spectral theorem is a secondary gap (less leveraged because VERIFIER's structural R3.5 sidesteps it via spectral-image-level reformulation).
