# Reading notes: Bhatt-Morrow-Scholze, *Integral p-adic Hodge theory* (BMS1)

> Folder `references/01_prismatic_cohomology/`. The BMS1 paper the direction docs cite
> (R5 / Direction 3). This is the precursor that prismatic cohomology later abstracts:
> it constructs, for proper smooth formal schemes over O_C (C a complete algebraically
> closed nonarchimedean extension of Q_p), a cohomology theory **A-Omega_X /
> RGamma_A_inf** valued in Fontaine's period ring A_inf = W(O_C^flat), carrying a
> Frobenius phi, that specializes to crystalline, de Rham, and etale cohomology at
> once. The technical heart is the **decalage (L-eta) operator**, which kills "junk
> torsion." It is the **single-place (one C, one perfect prism A_inf)** ancestor of the
> absolute theory. Read after Bhatt-Scholze and Bhatt-Lurie (those are the abstract
> framework; this is the concrete A_inf model the framework was built to explain).

## One-line takeaway

There is one cohomology theory RGamma_A_inf(X), living over all of Spec(A_inf), with a
Frobenius phi that becomes an isomorphism after inverting xi (a generator of
ker(theta : A_inf -> O_C)); restricting it to various big subsets of Spec(A_inf)
recovers crystalline (base change to W(k)), de Rham (base change along theta), and
etale (invert mu) cohomology. Its cohomology groups are **Breuil-Kisin-Fargues
modules**, the mixed-characteristic analogue of Dieudonne modules, classified by
Fargues as pairs (T, Xi) of a Z_p-lattice and a B_dR-lattice. The construction uses the
**L-eta operator** to remove junk torsion, and the affine pieces are computed by the
**q-de Rham complex** (Theorem 1.17). The headline arithmetic consequence: crystalline
torsion bounds etale torsion length-by-length (Theorem 1.1(ii)), so p-torsion in
char-p de Rham cohomology is forced by p-torsion in char-0 singular homology.

## Technical content (section by section)

### §1.1 Background: Grothendieck's mysterious functor

The central problem: relate p-adic etale cohomology H^i_et(X_Kbar, Z_p) of the generic
fiber to crystalline cohomology H^i_crys(X_k / W(k)) of the special fiber. Fontaine's
conjecture (proved by Tsuji et al.) gives a phi, G_K-equivariant iso after tensoring
with B_crys; but integrally, for small p or ramified K, the integral structure is not
preserved. BMS1 lifts the restriction.

### §1.2 The main comparison theorem and the torsion inequality

**Theorem 1.1.** Let X be a proper smooth formal scheme over O_K (K a complete
discretely valued nonarchimedean extension of Q_p, perfect residue field k), C a
completed algebraic closure, X_C the geometric rigid-analytic generic fiber. Then for
all i >= 0:
(i) (comparison) H^i_et(X_C, Z_p) tensor_{Z_p} B_crys = H^i_crys(X_k/W(k)) tensor B_crys,
    compatible with Galois, Frobenius, filtration; so H^i_et(X_C, Q_p) is crystalline.
(ii) (torsion inequality) length_{W(k)}(H^i_crys(X_k/W(k))_tors / p^n)
    >= length_{Z_p}(H^i_et(X_C, Z_p)_tors / p^n) for all n; in particular
    dim_k H^i_dR(X) >= dim_{F_p} H^i_et(X_C, F_p).
(iii) if crystalline cohomology in degrees i, i+1 is torsion-free, the crystalline
    H^i with its phi is recovered functorially from the etale H^i.
Remark 1.2: the inequality "explains" pathologies. An Enriques surface in char 2 has
H^1_dR != 0 (unlike any other characteristic); since the etale H^1(S_C, F_2) = F_2 != 0
from pi_1 = Z/2, the inequality (1) forces H^1_dR(S_k) != 0. Remark 1.3 shows the
inequality is sharp and cannot be upgraded to a subquotient relation.

### §1.2 A_inf and its special elements

A_inf = W(O^flat), O^flat = lim_phi O/p the tilt, so Frobenius on O^flat is bijective
and A_inf has a Frobenius automorphism phi. Fix a compatible system of primitive
p-power roots of unity zeta_{p^r}; epsilon = (1, zeta_p, ...) in O^flat,
mu = [epsilon] - 1, and
  xi = mu / phi^{-1}(mu) = ([epsilon] - 1)/([epsilon]^{1/p} - 1) = sum_{i=0}^{p-1} [epsilon]^{i/p}.
theta : A_inf -> O has kernel generated by the non-zero-divisor xi. A_crys = p-adic
completion of the PD-envelope of A_inf along ker theta; B_crys = A_crys[1/mu];
B_dR^+ = xi-adic completion of A_inf[1/p] (a complete DVR with uniformizer xi, residue
field C). The map phi shifts the scale: phi : A_inf[1/xi] = A_inf[1/phi^{-1}(xi)].

### §1.2 Breuil-Kisin-Fargues modules and Fargues' classification

**Definition 1.5 (BKF module):** a finitely presented A_inf-module M with a phi-linear
isomorphism phi_M : M[1/xi] = M[1/phi(xi)], such that M[1/p] is finite free. This is the
mixed-characteristic analogue of a Dieudonne module ("mixed-characteristic local
shtuka"). **Theorem 1.6 (Fargues):** the category of finite-free BKF modules is
equivalent to pairs (T, Xi), T a finite-free Z_p-module, Xi a B_dR^+-lattice in
T tensor_{Z_p} B_dR. Used (with the de Rham comparison Theorem 1.7 supplying the
B_dR^+-lattice Xi = H^i_dR(X/B_dR^+)) to formulate the recovery in Theorem 1.4.

### §1.2 / §1.3 The cohomology theory A-Omega and the comparisons (Thm 1.8, 1.10)

**Theorem 1.8.** For X proper smooth over O (C algebraically closed): there is a
perfect complex RGamma_A_inf(X) of A_inf-modules with phi-linear phi inducing
RGamma_A_inf(X)[1/xi] = RGamma_A_inf(X)[1/phi(xi)], all cohomology groups BKF modules,
and:
(i) base change to W(k): RGamma_A_inf tensor^L W(k) = RGamma_crys(X_k/W(k));
(ii) base change along theta: RGamma_A_inf tensor^L_A_inf O = RGamma_dR(X);
(iii) base change to A_crys: = RGamma_crys(X_{O/p}/A_crys);
(iv) invert mu: RGamma_A_inf tensor A_inf[1/mu] = RGamma_et(X, Z_p) tensor Z_p A_inf[1/mu].
"One cohomology theory which lives over all of Spec A_inf, described over various big
subsets by the classical theories." The complex is built (Definition 1.12) as
  A-Omega_X := L eta_mu (R nu_* A_inf,X),
nu : X_proet -> X_Zar the nearby-cycles projection.

### §1.3 / §6 The L-eta (decalage) operator

For f a non-zero-divisor and D a complex of f-torsion-free A-modules,
(eta_f D)^i = { x in f^i D^i : dx in f^{i+1} D^{i+1} }, a subcomplex of D[1/f]. It
passes to a non-exact derived functor L eta_f with
H^i(eta_f D) = H^i(D)/H^i(D)[f] (Remark 1.13). The effect: it **kills f-torsion on
cohomology**, removing the "junk torsion" that defeated Faltings' earlier approach.
The key Lemma 1.16: L eta_mu turns the almost-quasi-isomorphism (Faltings almost purity)
  RGamma_cont(Gamma, A_inf(R_infty)) -> RGamma_proet(X, A_inf,X)
into an honest quasi-isomorphism. L eta also appeared in Berthelot-Ogus / Illusie's
work relating Hodge and Newton polygons (Remark 1.14).

### §1.3 / §8-10 The q-de Rham computation (Theorem 1.17)

For R = O<T^{+-1}> (a torus), A-Omega_R is computed by the **q-de Rham complex**
  A_inf<T^{+-1}> --(d_q / d_q T)--> A_inf<T^{+-1}>, T^i -> [i]_q T^{i-1}, q = [epsilon],
where d_q f / d_q T = (f(qT) - f(T))/(qT - T) is a finite q-difference quotient. In d
variables, formally etale maps deform uniquely, with commuting q-derivations
d_q/d_q T_i = (gamma_i - 1)/(q T_i - T_i), gamma_i the deformed automorphism. Setting
q = 1 recovers the usual de Rham complex. The q-de Rham complex is an E_infinity-algebra
but its DGA structure is noncommutative (the Leibniz rule twists by gamma_i), so
commutativity holds only up to homotopy (Remark 1.18). The comparison to Langer-Zink's
relative de Rham-Witt complex (Lemma 1.19: H^i(W_r Omega) = W_r Omega^{i,cont}_{R/O})
identifies A-Omega with the de Rham-Witt complex, recovering the Cartier isomorphism in
the limit r -> infinity (Remark 1.20).

### §1.4 Genesis: THH

The starting point (their own account): Hesselholt computed topological cyclic homology
TC of O_{C_p} with the answer being the BKF version of Tate twists; this suggested a
graded-piece of topological Frobenius homology computing the sought theory. The L-eta
operator was rediscovered in analyzing the expected properties of A-Omega_R. (The
THH-based construction is the subject of BMS2; the footnote-in-print notes the prismatic
site of [Bhatt-Scholze] later gives the cleanest construction.)

## Points mapped to the project

1. **One theory over Spec(A_inf), specializing everywhere by restriction (Thm 1.8,
   1.10).** RGamma_A_inf(X) is a perfect complex with phi; the comparisons are base
   changes along maps out of A_inf (to W(k), theta, A_crys, invert mu). One object, all
   the classical theories as restrictions.
   -> This "one object over a base, all known theories are its restrictions" is the
   template the program wants for **a single cohomology of the arithmetic surface whose
   restrictions to each place give the local data**. A_inf is the single-place (one C)
   case; Bhatt-Lurie's WCart is the all-primes globalization. BMS1 is the
   proof-of-concept that the unification is real and computable.

2. **A Frobenius phi exists though X carries none, and it comes from the tilt (Remark
   1.9, Thm 1.8).** No Frobenius on X (char 0), yet RGamma_A_inf(X) carries phi from the
   Frobenius on the tilt O^flat (the perfect prism A_inf); phi is an iso after inverting
   xi.
   -> This is the structural precedent for putting a **Frobenius correspondence Gamma_S
   on an object with no obvious self-map**. Spec(Z) x Spec(Z) has no geometric Frobenius
   the way a curve over F_q does; BMS1 shows the Frobenius can come from the
   **coefficient ring's tilt / delta-structure**, not from the scheme. This is the
   mechanism by which 2Q's place-dependent Gamma_S can exist at all: it lives on the
   coefficients (A_inf, then WCart), not on the base.

3. **The scale element xi and its companion mu; Frobenius shifts the scale (Thm 1.4,
   §1.2).** xi generates ker(theta); mu = [epsilon] - 1; inverting mu gives etale, base
   change along theta gives de Rham; phi : A_inf[1/xi] = A_inf[1/phi^{-1}(xi)].
   -> xi is the **single per-place scale**, the A_inf analogue of the function field's q
   (and of [p]_q in the q-de Rham prism). 2Q's central finding is that over Spec(Z) the
   scale must be **place-dependent**, one xi_p per prime rather than one global q. BMS1
   gives one xi for one place C; the program needs the family {xi_p}_p plus the
   archimedean factor (2I). The relation phi(xi) (Frobenius shifts the scale) is the
   local form of the multiplicative-vs-additive tension Leichtnam called "of arithmetic
   nature," and it is the same scale-shift that, in Bhatt-Lurie, the Sen operator Theta
   differentiates.

4. **Cohomology groups are Breuil-Kisin-Fargues modules = mixed-char Dieudonne modules
   = shtukas (Def 1.5, Thm 1.6 Fargues).** A BKF module is a lattice plus a
   phi-linear isomorphism away from the scale (M[1/xi] = M[1/phi(xi)]); Fargues
   classifies the finite-free ones as pairs (T, Xi).
   -> A BKF module is a **lattice plus a Frobenius-semilinear isomorphism meromorphic
   along a divisor**: the linear-algebra shadow of a Frobenius correspondence on
   cohomology, and exactly the function-field-shtuka structure 2G/2Q want to lift. This
   is the local model for what Gamma_S induces on the program's H^*. The Fargues pair
   (T, Xi) = (etale lattice, de Rham lattice) is the precise data the program's
   "correspondence on cohomology" would reduce to at each place.

5. **Crystalline torsion bounds etale torsion; p-torsion is forced (Thm 1.1(ii), eq
   (1)).** length(H^i_crys tors / p^n) >= length(H^i_et tors / p^n), giving
   dim_k H^i_dR >= dim_{F_p} H^i_et. p-torsion classes in char-0 homology force nonzero
   de Rham cohomology mod p (Enriques surface, Remark 1.2).
   -> This is an **inequality between two cohomological dimensions extracted from one
   Frobenius-equipped object**. It is not a signature, but it is the same *genre* as
   Direction 8: a comparison inequality about cohomology forced by the arithmetic
   structure. Worth keeping as a worked example of "the integral structure forces an
   inequality between cohomologies," the flavor (not the content) of a Hodge-index
   argument. The sharpness results (Remark 1.3, no subquotient upgrade) are a caution:
   integral structure forces inequalities but not the cleaner subobject relations a
   naive index argument might hope for.

## What this changes for the program

- **BMS1 is the concrete single-place model; the abstract framework (Bhatt-Scholze,
  Bhatt-Lurie) is what to cite for Spec(Z).** For the live front the relevant uses are:
  (a) it proves the unification "one Frobenius-equipped cohomology, all classical
  theories by restriction" is achievable and computable (the q-de Rham complex);
  (b) it shows the Frobenius comes from the coefficient ring's tilt, the mechanism that
  lets Gamma_S exist on an object with no geometric self-map; (c) BKF modules / shtukas
  are the local linear-algebra model of what Gamma_S induces on H^*.

- **It pins the per-place scale (xi) that 2Q says must become place-dependent, and the
  q-de Rham complex is the explicit deformation.** BMS1 is one place; the program's task
  is the family over all p plus infinity. The q-de Rham complex (Theorem 1.17) is the
  explicit one-parameter (q-)deformation whose symmetry the Sen flow later integrates;
  this note's job is to mark BMS1 as the local building block, computed by an explicit
  q-difference complex, not the global object.

- **The L-eta operator is the technical device that makes integral comparison work.**
  Worth flagging for the program: the obstruction to a clean integral theory was "junk
  torsion," removed by the decalage L eta_mu. Any program-side attempt to build an
  integral cohomology of the arithmetic surface will face the analogous torsion-control
  problem, and L-eta is the proven tool (also the source of the Nygaard isogeny in the
  prismatic theory).

- **No flow, no signature here.** BMS1 has the Frobenius phi but not the continuous
  generator (that is the Sen operator in Bhatt-Lurie) and not an intersection form. The
  q-de Rham deformation parameter q is the discrete shadow of the flow; its symmetry
  group (1 + pZ_p)^x and infinitesimal generator (the Sen operator) live in the absolute
  theory. Its contribution to Direction 8 is only the genre (an inequality between
  cohomologies forced by integral arithmetic structure), not a construction.
