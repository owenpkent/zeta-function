# Reading notes: Bhatt-Morrow-Scholze, *Topological Hochschild homology and integral p-adic Hodge theory* (BMS2)

> Folder `references/01_prismatic_cohomology/`. The BMS2 paper: the THH / motivic
> filtration precursor to prismatic cohomology. It reconstructs the BMS1 A_inf-cohomology
> (and a Breuil-Kisin refinement over O_K, K discretely valued) out of **topological
> cyclic homology** TC, equipping everything with a **motivic-style filtration** whose
> graded pieces are the cohomology, and defining the **syntomic complexes Z_p(n)** as
> Frobenius-fixed-point fibers. For the program the load-bearing facts are: (a) the
> Frobenius on the cohomology is the same Frobenius as the **cyclotomic Frobenius**
> phi_p on THH, the ultimate source of the phi in all these theories; (b) the Nygaard
> filtration appears here as a complete filtration coming from the Postnikov/even
> filtration of TC^-/TP, the grading a flow or a signature needs; (c) Z_p(n) =
> hofib(phi - can), the trace-formula skeleton. Read last of the four prismatic sources
> (it is the homotopy-theoretic backstory; the prismatic site reproves its main theorem
> more cleanly, per Remark 1.3).

## One-line takeaway

THH(A) is a circle-equivariant E_infinity-ring spectrum carrying a **cyclotomic
Frobenius** phi_p : THH(A) -> THH(A)^{tC_p}; from it one forms TC^- = THH^{hT} and TP =
THH^{tT}, and pi_0 TC^-(A) acquires a Frobenius endomorphism phi that is "the ultimate
source" of the Frobenius on BMS1's A_inf-cohomology (Hesselholt: pi_0 TC^-(R) = A_inf(R)
for perfectoid R, Theorem 1.6). Building a **motivic / Postnikov (even) filtration** on
TC^-, TP, THH (Theorem 1.12) yields complete exhaustive filtrations whose graded pieces
are the prismatic cohomology twists Prism-hat_A{n}[2n], whose associated Nygaard
filtration N^{>=*} matches the classical one, and from which **syntomic cohomology**
Z_p(n) = hofib(phi - can) is defined. So the Frobenius and the Nygaard grading both
come, at bottom, from algebraic topology.

## Technical content (section by section)

### §1.1 The Breuil-Kisin refinement (Theorem 1.2)

For X proper smooth over O_K (K discretely valued over Q_p, perfect residue field k),
fix a uniformizer omega; the Breuil-Kisin ring is S = W(k)[[z]] with theta-bar : S -> O_K
sending z to omega, kernel generated by an Eisenstein polynomial E(z), and Frobenius phi
on S (Frobenius on W(k), z -> z^p). **Definition 1.1 (Breuil-Kisin module):** a finitely
generated S-module M with M tensor_{S, phi} S[1/E] = M[1/E].
**Theorem 1.2.** There is an S-linear cohomology theory RGamma_S(X) with phi-linear phi:
(1) base extension to A_inf recovers RGamma_A_inf(X_{O_C}), phi an iso after inverting E,
    all H^i_S(X) Breuil-Kisin modules, etale after scalar extension to A_inf[1/mu];
(2) base change along theta = theta-bar phi : S -> O_K recovers RGamma_dR(X/O_K);
(3) base change along S -> W(k) (Frobenius on W(k), z -> 0) recovers
    RGamma_crys(X_k/W(k)).
Remark 1.3: the prismatic site reproves Theorem 1.2 without THH. Remark 1.4: the
Frobenius twists are not artifacts; they force the length of each H^i_dR(X/O_K)_tors to
be a multiple of p (each indecomposable summand has length a multiple of p).

### §1.2 THH and the cyclotomic Frobenius

THH(A) (Bokstedt): replace Z by the sphere spectrum S in the Hochschild complex. THH(A)
is a T = S^1-equivariant E_infinity-ring spectrum with a T/C_p-equivariant **cyclotomic
Frobenius** phi_p : THH(A) -> THH(A)^{tC_p}, where (-)^{tC_p} is the Tate construction
(cone of the norm map (-)_{hC_p} -> (-)^{hC_p}). These Frobenius maps are essential and
genuinely topological: provably absent from algebraic Hochschild homology. phi_p comes
(in the commutative case) from the Tate diagonal A -> (A tensor ... tensor A)^{tC_p}
(NS18). Forming TC^-(A) = THH(A)^{hT} (homotopy fixed points) and TP(A) = THH(A)^{tT}
(Tate, periodic), there is a canonical map can : TC^-(A) -> TP(A) and a Frobenius
phi : TC^-(A; Z_p) -> TP(A; Z_p) induced by phi_p (using the equivalence
(THH^{tC_p})^{hT} = TP, NS18 Lemma II.4.2). **pi_0 TC^-(A; Z_p) acquires the Frobenius
phi that is the ultimate source of the endomorphism of RGamma_S(X)** and is a lift of
Frobenius mod p (Remark 1.5: only Bokstedt's pi_* THH(F_p) = F_p[u] is needed beyond
formal facts). Hesselholt (Theorem 1.6): pi_0 TC^-(R; Z_p) = A_inf(R) for perfectoid R,
phi-equivariantly. This is the starting point.

### §1.3 From THH to Breuil-Kisin: quasisyntomic descent

**Definition 1.7 (quasisyntomic site):** a ring A is quasisyntomic if p-complete, with
bounded p^infinity-torsion, and L_{A/Z_p} tensor^L A/pA has Tor-amplitude in [-1, 0]; a
map is quasisyntomic (cover) if A/p^n A -> B/p^n B is flat (faithfully flat) and the
relative cotangent complex has Tor-amplitude in [-1, 0]. QSyn contains all p-complete
regular rings, p-complete local complete intersections, perfectoid rings, and p-adic
completions of smooth O_C-algebras. The presheaf B -> pi_0 TC^-(B; Z_p) is a sheaf on a
base of **quasiregular semiperfectoid** rings with vanishing higher cohomology (Remark
1.9). **Theorem 1.8:** A-Omega = RGamma_syn(A, pi_0 TC^-(-; Z_p)); so A-Omega is
recovered by flat descent from THH. **Theorem 1.10** (char p): RGamma_crys(A/W(k)) =
RGamma_syn(A, pi_0 TC^-(-; Z_p)). The sheaf pi_0 TC^- is identified with the Nygaard
completion of A_crys (Remark 1.11), and the Nygaard filtration N^{>=*} A-hat_A (from the
homotopy-fixed-point spectral sequence's abutment filtration) is identified with the
classical Nygaard filtration; the notation A-hat anticipates the prismatic cohomology of
[Bhatt-Scholze].

### §1.4 The motivic filtration (Theorem 1.12)

For A quasisyntomic, the Postnikov/even filtration gives **motivic filtrations**:
  Fil^n THH(A; Z_p) = RGamma_syn(A, tau_{>=2n} THH(-; Z_p)), similarly TC^- and TP,
complete exhaustive decreasing Z-indexed multiplicative filtrations (Theorem 1.12(2)),
with graded pieces (1.12(4)):
  gr^n THH = N^n A-hat_A [2n], gr^n TC^- = N^{>=n} A-hat_A{n} [2n],
  gr^n TP = A-hat_A{n} [2n],
where A-hat_A = gr^0 TC^- = gr^0 TP is the filtered E_infinity-ring with its Nygaard
filtration N^{>=*}, and A-hat_A{1} = gr^1 TP[-2] is the (invertible) Breuil-Kisin twist.
These give multiplicative spectral sequences (e.g. E_2^{ij} = H^{i-j}(N^{>=-j} A-hat{-j})
=> pi_{-i-j} TC^-). The whole package is "reminiscent of the motivic filtration on
algebraic K-theory" (FS02); rationally it is the Adams-eigenspace filtration
(Adams operation psi_m acts by m^n on gr^n, Prop 9.14).

### §1.4 Syntomic cohomology Z_p(n) as a Frobenius-fixed-point fiber (Thm 1.12(5))

The Frobenius phi : TC^- -> TP induces phi : Fil^n TC^- -> Fil^n TP, giving
  Fil^n TC^-(A; Z_p) = hofib(phi - can : Fil^n TC^- -> Fil^n TP),
  TC(A; Z_p) = hofib(phi - can : TC^- -> TP),
with graded pieces the **syntomic complexes**:
  Z_p(n)(A) := gr^n TC(A; Z_p)[-2n] = hofib(phi - can : N^{>=n} A-hat{n} -> A-hat{n}),
where phi : N^{>=n} A-hat{n} -> A-hat{n} is a divided Frobenius (Remark 1.14), a version
of syntomic cohomology. Z_p(n) is locally on QSyn concentrated in degrees 0, 1; in
degree 0, Z_p(0) = Z_p (constant); in degree 1, **Z_p(1) = T_p G_m [0]** (the p-adic Tate
module of G_m); for n < 0, Z_p(n) = 0. In char p the trace from K-theory gives
K_{2n}(-; Z_p) = Z_p(n). **Theorem 1.15** identifies Z_p(n): for smooth A over a perfect
field k, Z_p(n) = W Omega^n_{X, log}[-n] (logarithmic de Rham-Witt); for A a smooth
O_C-algebra, Z_p(n) = tau^{<=n} R psi Z_p(n) (truncated p-adic nearby cycles). A
comparison with Schneider's sheaves and the Lichtenbaum-Quillen conjecture follows
(Remark 1.16, the Hesselholt-Madsen filtration on K(K; Z_p)).

### §1.5 Complements on cyclic homology (Theorem 1.17)

The same machine on usual HH/HC^-/HP gives Fil^n HC^- = RGamma_syn(A, tau_{>=2n} HC^-)
with gr^n HC^- = L-Omega-hat^{>=n}_{A/R}[2n] (Hodge-completed derived de Rham), the
algebraic analogue of the motivic filtration.

## Points mapped to the project

1. **The Frobenius on the cohomology is the cyclotomic Frobenius of THH (§1.2-1.3, Thm
   1.6).** phi_p : THH(A) -> THH(A)^{tC_p} is an essential topological feature (absent
   from algebraic HH); it induces phi on pi_0 TC^-(A; Z_p) = pi_0 TP(A; Z_p), the
   ultimate source of the phi on RGamma_S(X), and a lift of Frobenius mod p. Hesselholt's
   pi_0 TC^-(R) = A_inf(R) for perfectoid R is the base case.
   -> This traces the program's wanted Frobenius (Gamma_S) all the way down to a
   topological origin. The chain is: cyclotomic Frobenius on THH -> phi on TC^-/TP -> phi
   on A_inf-cohomology (BMS1) -> phi on prismatic cohomology / F on WCart (Bhatt-Lurie).
   For 2Q the takeaway is that the Frobenius is **forced and canonical at every level**,
   not a choice; and the per-prime structure is built in, since THH gives one cyclotomic
   Frobenius per prime p (the C_p-Tate construction phi_p). The place-dependence 2Q needs
   is engineered on top of this canonical per-p phi_p, which is itself the per-prime
   structure the program wants assembled.

2. **The Nygaard/motivic filtration: the grading for both Direction 4 and 8, with
   explicit graded pieces (Thm 1.12, Remark 1.11).** The Postnikov/even filtration on
   TC^-/TP gives a complete exhaustive multiplicative filtration with
   gr^n TC^- = N^{>=n} A-hat{n}[2n], gr^n TP = A-hat{n}[2n], and the abutment Nygaard
   filtration matches the classical one.
   -> A **graded structure on the cohomology from a filtration with a clean spectral
   sequence**, shown to be the even/motivic filtration of a K-theory-like object (not ad
   hoc). This is the same Nygaard grading Bhatt-Lurie's Sen operator acts on diagonally
   (by -n), and whose connecting map is Theta. For Direction 8 it is where a cup-product
   / Hodge-star grading would come from; for Direction 4 it is the filtration
   det(s - Theta) would be taken over. The Breuil-Kisin twist A-hat{1} = gr^1 TP[-2] is
   the invertible "scale line bundle" (O_WCart{1} in the absolute theory).

3. **Z_p(n) = hofib(phi - can): the trace-formula / regularized-determinant skeleton
   (Thm 1.12(5)).** TC = hofib(phi - can : TC^- -> TP) and Z_p(n) =
   hofib(phi - can : N^{>=n} A-hat{n} -> A-hat{n}); TP = THH^{tT} (periodic) and
   TC^- = THH^{hT} (homotopy fixed points), with the circle action T = S^1.
   -> The "fixed points of Frobenius / fiber of (phi - can)" shape is the trace-formula /
   regularized-determinant skeleton (Direction 4.6, det(s - Phi)). 2R computed
   -zeta'/zeta = sum Lambda(n) n^{-s} as a dynamical-zeta log-derivative; the periodic-
   cyclic / (phi - can)-fiber machinery is the homotopy-theoretic home where such a trace
   of Frobenius lives. The **circle action T = S^1 on THH is itself a continuous
   one-parameter symmetry**, the closest thing in this paper to a flow; the Sen operator
   of the absolute theory is the infinitesimal generator that this circle action
   foreshadows. That Z_p(1) = T_p G_m connects the n=1 syntomic complex to the
   multiplicative group, matching the prismatic logarithm and the program's interest in
   G_m-valued / multiplicative correspondences.

4. **Everything is assembled by quasisyntomic flat descent from semiperfectoid rings
   (Def 1.7, §1.4, Remark 1.9).** The quasisyntomic site qSyn has a base of quasiregular
   semiperfectoid rings on which pi_0 TC^-(-; Z_p) is a sheaf with vanishing higher
   cohomology; A-Omega and the filtrations are recovered by descent (Theorem 1.8).
   -> This descent-from-perfectoid pattern is the same move BMS1 / Bhatt-Scholze use
   (perfect prisms = perfectoid rings as the computational base). It reinforces that the
   **right base for these constructions is perfectoid / semiperfectoid**, the
   F_1-flavored "maximally ramified" objects; the arithmetic surface's base point (2K)
   should be sought in that world, consistent with Bhatt-Lurie's WCart being built from
   Cartier-Witt divisors on such rings.

5. **The Breuil-Kisin refinement over discretely valued O_K (Thm 1.2).** RGamma_S(X)
   over S = W(k)[[z]] with phi-linear Frobenius recovers A_inf-cohomology after base
   change, de Rham along theta, crystalline of the special fiber along z -> 0. Frobenius
   twists (Remark 1.4) force torsion in de Rham cohomology to have length a multiple of p.
   -> The Breuil-Kisin ring W(k)[[z]] is a **non-perfect prism with an explicit
   uniformizer-deformation z**: the discrete deformation parameter that, in the absolute
   theory, becomes the Sen/Nygaard direction. Another instance of the per-place "scale
   with a deformation parameter," the local raw material for a global flow. The
   "length a multiple of p" torsion constraint (Remark 1.4) is the BMS2 form of BMS1's
   torsion inequality, again the genre of an integral-structure-forced cohomological
   constraint (Direction 8's flavor).

## What this changes for the program

- **The Frobenius is canonical all the way down to topology.** BMS2 shows phi originates
  in the cyclotomic Frobenius phi_p of THH. Reassurance for the Gamma_S program: the
  Frobenius the construction needs is forced, not a free choice. The work 2Q identifies
  (making it place-dependent / gluing over all primes and infinity) is the genuine
  remaining content, not the existence of phi. And the per-prime phi_p (one C_p-Tate
  construction per p) is exactly the one-scale-per-prime structure 2Q wants assembled.

- **The Nygaard/motivic filtration is the grading for both Direction 4 and 8, and it is
  the even filtration of a K-theory-like object.** BMS2 is where this filtration is shown
  structural (Postnikov/even, multiplicative, complete, with a clean spectral sequence),
  with explicit graded pieces N^{>=n} A-hat{n}[2n]. A regularized determinant
  (Direction 4.6) or a cup-product signature (Direction 8) would both be defined relative
  to this grading; BMS2 certifies it is not ad hoc and supplies the invertible scale
  twist A-hat{1}.

- **Fixed-point / periodic constructions are the trace-formula home, with the circle
  action as the proto-flow.** TC^-, TP, and Z_p(n) = hofib(phi - can) are the homotopy-
  theoretic skeleton of the Lefschetz / det(s - Phi) statement 2R and Direction 4.6 want.
  No trace formula for zeta is proved here, but the genus matches, and the S^1 = T action
  on THH is the continuous symmetry whose generator (the Sen operator, in the absolute
  theory) is the flow Direction 4 needs. Z_p(1) = T_p G_m additionally ties the syntomic
  n=1 level to the multiplicative group / prismatic logarithm.

- **Still no signature, and this is the furthest-upstream source.** BMS2 is the backstory
  (THH), not the front. It has Frobenius and grading but no intersection form, no flow
  with zeta-zero spectrum, and (being over O_K / Z_p) no archimedean place. The torsion-
  length constraint (Remark 1.4) is the closest thing to a cohomological inequality, the
  flavor not the content of a Hodge-index argument. For the live front, cite Bhatt-Lurie
  (absolute, Sen operator, contracting Frobenius) as the working substrate and BMS2 as
  the proof that its Frobenius (cyclotomic) and Nygaard grading (motivic/even) are
  canonical and topological in origin.
