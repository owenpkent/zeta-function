# Research Directions for the RH Proof Program

> Eight focused research directions identified by the project as the out-of-scope, research-grade work that would close the proof program described in [`../proof_program.md`](../proof_program.md). Each direction is documented with: problem statement, what "done" looks like, prerequisites, sub-problems, falsifiability, estimated effort, connections, references.
>
> These are not project-internal experiments. They are research programs requiring expert mathematicians at the postdoc-or-faculty level, operating over years. The documents here are the operational specifications that would allow such mathematicians (or research groups) to begin.

### Speculative directions 9-13 (proposed 2026-05-30)

Five "crazy but formal" directions generated by attacking the program's single sharpest finding head-on: the **place-dependent-bidegree obstruction** (LEARNINGS #25, the unique fact that breaks the function-field template over $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$). Each proposes a specific new object answering "what single structure carries the continuum of commensurable scales $\{\log p\}$ at once, and supports a signature (not a trace)?" Each is tagged against kill criteria K1 (signature not trace), K2 (must exclude Davenport-Heilbronn), K3 (must specialize to Weil 1948). Directions 9 and 13 ship with same-day falsification experiments.

| # | Direction | Doc | Attacks #25 by | Quick falsification? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | Arithmetic matroid: Li positivity as a Hodge-Riemann relation | [`09_arithmetic_matroid_li.md`](09_arithmetic_matroid_li.md) | matroid encodes Euler product as independence structure | yes ([`e3n_li_signature.py`](../../../experiments/positivity/e3n_li_signature.py)) |
| 10 | Cyclotomic Frobenius over the sphere spectrum (THH/TC) | [`10_thh_cyclotomic_frobenius.md`](10_thh_cyclotomic_frobenius.md) | one circle action carries all primes at once (dissolves #25 at root) | theory note |
| 11 | Bidegree-as-a-measure (idele-class-valued intersection theory) | [`11_bidegree_as_measure.md`](11_bidegree_as_measure.md) | makes the place-dependent bidegree the definition (a measure) | reuse 3J/3M |
| 12 | RH as exact criticality (de Bruijn-Newman $\Lambda = 0$) | [`12_debruijn_newman_criticality.md`](12_debruijn_newman_criticality.md) | promotes the marginal-positivity thesis to a criticality theorem | reuse E0-E3 |
| 13 | Infinite-genus tau-function (Riemann bilinear relations) | [`13_infinite_genus_tau_function.md`](13_infinite_genus_tau_function.md) | embraces infinite genus; periods replace finite intersection form | yes ([`e_jensen_turan.py`](../../../experiments/integrable/e_jensen_turan.py)) |

Directions 9 and 13 meet at the **Turan inequalities / Jensen-polynomial** framework (the degree-1 Hodge-Riemann content = log-concavity = real-rootedness). Direction 10 is the most likely to be structurally right (it dissolves #25 rather than working around it) and the most demanding. These are lower-confidence than 1-8 and explicitly speculative; their near-term value is the two cheap D-H discriminator experiments and the structural re-pointing of Direction 3 toward TC over the sphere spectrum.

**Synthesis (2026-05-30): all roads lead to the signature.** After pushing Direction 10 to Hesselholt's THH/Hasse-Weil determinant theorem (a survey, LEARNINGS #29) and a cross-direction consolidation ([`../all_roads_to_the_signature.md`](../all_roads_to_the_signature.md), LEARNINGS #30), the conclusion is that every framework (Arch 1-4, Directions 8/9/10/13) supplies the *realization* of zeta (a determinant/trace), but RH is the *signature/positivity*, the same object in every framework, and the irreducible content. Hesselholt's TP odd/even split is exactly Weil's primitive/hyperbolic Hodge-index split (verified in the function-field case, [`../../../experiments/arithmetic_geometric/e2s_tp_hodge_split.py`](../../../experiments/arithmetic_geometric/e2s_tp_hodge_split.py)). Operational steer: concentrate on the signature (Direction 8 / form 10A.iii), not on more realizations.

## Reading order

For someone choosing what to work on:

- If you have background in **arithmetic geometry / F_1**: directions 1, 2, 4, 8.
- If you have background in **noncommutative geometry / Connes program**: directions 2, 5.
- If you have background in **prismatic cohomology / p-adic Hodge theory**: directions 3, 4.
- If you have background in **tropical geometry**: directions 5, 8.
- If you have background in **analytic number theory** (V-K, MT, exponential sums): directions 6, 7.

The most central direction is **#8: the Hodge index theorem on a constructed surface**. Directions 1-4 build the surface and surrounding infrastructure; direction 8 is the actual positivity theorem that closes RH. Direction 5 is a supporting infrastructure piece. Direction 6 is an alternative escape from the LP/SDP family; direction 7 is a partial improvement to the V-K zero-free region constant (which alone does not prove RH but is independently valuable).

## Per-direction probability rationale

Each direction's success probability reflects:
- Existing partial progress in the literature (where there is more, probability is higher)
- The structural depth of the open problem (where it is shallower, probability is higher)
- The number of distinct attack angles available (where there are more, probability is higher)
- The historical track record of similar problems

Cumulative success probability for the FULL proof program (all directions executed successfully) is under 1%. Most directions, executed seriously, would produce major independent results even without the full proof.
