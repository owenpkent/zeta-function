# Direction 9: The arithmetic matroid (Li-coefficient positivity as a Hodge-Riemann relation)

> **Status: speculative, proposed 2026-05-30.** A "crazy but formal" direction generated by stress-testing the program against its own sharpest finding (the place-dependent-bidegree obstruction, LEARNINGS #25). First falsification experiment landed alongside this doc: [`e3n_li_signature.py`](../../../experiments/positivity/e3n_li_signature.py).
>
> **Headline.** Do not build a surface and hope it is tropical. Build a *matroid* directly on the primes whose Hodge-Riemann log-concavity (Adiprasito-Huh-Katz 2018) is exactly the Li-coefficient positivity $\lambda_n \ge 0$. This inverts Direction 8 attack-angle 4.A.

## 1. The idea in one paragraph

The Adiprasito-Huh-Katz theorem (AHK 2018) proves the Hodge-Riemann relations for the Chow ring of an arbitrary matroid, an object with **no underlying variety**. Direction 8.A tries to lift AHK *onto* a constructed arithmetic surface and inherits all the difficulty of building that surface. Direction 9 goes the other way: construct a matroid (or a sequence of matroids) $M_n$ directly on the primes, whose reduced characteristic polynomial converges to the completed $\xi(s)$, and such that the AHK Hodge-Riemann relations on the Chow ring $A^\bullet(M_n)$ give the Li-coefficient positivity. RH would then follow from a **signature** (the Hodge-Riemann form is definite on a primitive subspace), never from a trace identity. This is the K1-clean route the program keeps asking for.

## 2. Formal kernel

Seek a sequence of (poly)matroids $M_n$ with:

- **Ground set** the primes $p \le P(n)$; **rank function** built from $p$-adic valuations / the local Euler factors $(1 - p^{-s})^{-1}$.
- **Characteristic polynomial** $\chi_{M_n}(t)$ whose coefficients, after the standard reduction, match the Taylor/Hadamard data of $\xi(s)$ to order $n$.
- **AHK conclusion**: the Hodge-Riemann relations hold on $A^\bullet(M_n)$. In degree $\le 1$ they assert the **log-concavity** of the coefficient sequence; the full bilinear HR form is **definite on the primitive part**.

The target identification:

$$\text{(AHK log-concavity / HR signature on } A^\bullet(M_n)) \;\Longrightarrow\; \lambda_n \ge 0 \quad (\text{Li's criterion}) \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \text{RH}.$$

AHK's content is precisely that the absolute Whitney numbers $|w_k|$ of a matroid are log-concave, $w_{k-1} w_{k+1} \le w_k^2$ (the Heron-Rota-Welsh conjecture, now a theorem). If the Li coefficients (or a HR-Gram matrix built from them) are realized as such an object, their positivity is forced by the signature.

## 3. Why it could break Davenport-Heilbronn (K2)

A matroid encodes the Euler product as its independence structure: the local factor at $p$ is the rank contribution of the prime-$p$ atom. Davenport-Heilbronn has no Euler product, hence **no matroid, no Chow ring, no Hodge-Riemann relation**. This is the combinatorial shadow of LEARNINGS #25/#26: "no Euler product $\Leftrightarrow$ no local bidegrees $\Leftrightarrow$ no $\Gamma_S$." The K2 break is structural and clean: the object simply fails to exist for D-H.

## 4. Kill-criteria status

- **K1 (signature not trace):** clean. Hodge-Riemann is a signature statement by definition.
- **K2 (must exclude D-H):** clean by construction (no Euler product $\Rightarrow$ no matroid).
- **K3 (specialize to Weil):** the matroid of a curve over $\mathbb{F}_q$ should recover the AHK/tropical Hodge index already used in Direction 8 milestone 5.5 (the $(1,k)$ signature on $\mathrm{NS}(C \times C)$). Verifying this specialization is milestone 9.3.

## 5. Falsification (the cheap, decisive first test)

This is the rare research direction with a same-day kill switch. The experiment [`e3n_li_signature.py`](../../../experiments/positivity/e3n_li_signature.py):

1. Computes the Li coefficients $\lambda_1, \dots, \lambda_N$ for $\zeta$ and for Davenport-Heilbronn at high precision.
2. Tests the AHK-style log-concavity inequalities $\lambda_n^2 \ge \lambda_{n-1}\lambda_{n+1}$ and the **signature** of the Hankel/HR-Gram matrix $H = [\lambda_{i+j-1}]$ (its number of positive vs negative eigenvalues).
3. Asks whether D-H **violates** exactly the inequalities $\zeta$ satisfies.

**Decision rule.** If $\zeta$ satisfies the HR signature and D-H violates it, Direction 9 has teeth and earns a real matroid-construction effort. If $\zeta$ itself fails the HR signature, the matroid hypothesis is dead. If neither discriminates at reachable $n$ (the likely outcome, given the D-H off-line perturbation lives at the $4\times 10^{-5}$ scale, LEARNINGS #18/#19 stealth-window), the finding is the same "marginal positivity / stealth window" wall in a new basis, which is itself a compass reading.

## 6. Milestones

- **9.1** Define $M_n$ and state the HR/signature condition precisely (~10 pp).
- **9.2** Prove $\chi_{M_n} \to \xi$ in the relevant sense, or find the obstruction (~20 pp).
- **9.3** Verify $\mathbb{F}_q$-curve specialization recovers AHK tropical Hodge index (K3) (~10 pp).
- **9.4** Derive $\lambda_n \ge 0$ from the HR signature (~10 pp).

## 7. Honest probability and prior art

Probability this closes RH: low (single digits), comparable to Direction 8.A. The value is two-fold: (i) it is *cheap to falsify* and may yield a new D-H discriminator sharper than the Gram/Schur family (LEARNINGS #19 closed that family as a disproof avenue, but a HR-signature object is a different invariant); (ii) it connects RH to the AHK/log-concavity revolution, where the Hodge-Riemann machinery is now a flexible, repeatedly-successful tool. The closest existing rigorous bridge is the **Jensen-polynomial / Turan-inequality** framework (Pólya; Csordas-Norfolk-Varga; Griffin-Ono-Rolen-Zagier 2019), which is log-concavity of the $\xi$ Taylor coefficients and is exactly the degree-1 Hodge-Riemann content. Direction 13 develops that bridge from the integrable-systems side; Directions 9 and 13 meet at the Turan inequalities.
