# Direction 10: The cyclotomic Frobenius over the sphere spectrum (one Frobenius for all primes)

> **Status: speculative, proposed 2026-05-30.** Generated by attacking the program's sharpest obstruction (LEARNINGS #25: the place-dependent bidegree) at its root.
>
> **Headline.** LEARNINGS #25 shows the obstruction is that a single Frobenius $x \mapsto x^q$ carries one scale, but $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$ needs all $\{\log p\}$ at once. Homotopy theory has exactly one object that does this: the **cyclotomic Frobenius** on $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$, carrying a Frobenius $\varphi_p$ for every $p$ simultaneously, all organized by one circle action $S^1 \curvearrowright \mathrm{THH}$. Re-point the prismatic work (Direction 3) from per-prime over $\mathbb{Z}_p$ to global over the sphere spectrum.

## 1. The idea in one paragraph

The genuine "field with one element" in brave-new-algebra is the **sphere spectrum** $\mathbb{S}$ (it is the initial $E_\infty$-ring; $\mathbb{Z}$ is genuinely an $\mathbb{S}$-algebra). So the product surface the program cannot build, $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z}) \times_{\mathbb{F}_1} \mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$, has a literal definition as $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z} \otimes_{\mathbb{S}} \mathbb{Z}) = \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$, topological Hochschild homology. $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$ carries an $S^1$-action and a cyclotomic structure (Nikolaus-Scholze), i.e. compatible Frobenii $\varphi_p$ for **all** $p$ at once. The continuous $R$-flow the program keeps deriving (LEARNINGS #25 consequence 3) is not a metaphor here: it **is** the circle action (or its universal cover $\mathbb{R} \to S^1$). The per-prime structure of finding #25 is recovered at the $C_p$-fixed points. The target theorem: the trace pairing on $\mathrm{TC}(\mathbb{Z})$ (topological cyclic homology) carries a Hermitian signature whose definiteness is RH, and the von Mangoldt prime sum of LEARNINGS #26 is the $S^1$-equivariant self-intersection $\Gamma_S^2$.

## 2. Why this is different from Direction 3 (prismatic over $\mathbb{Z}_p$)

Prismatic cohomology and the Bhatt-Scholze / Bhatt-Morrow-Scholze program work **one prime at a time**, over $\mathbb{Z}_p$ or a prism $(A, I)$. That is *precisely why* the program inherits the $(1,p)$-per-prime obstruction of finding #25: it never sees the primes together; there is no single object carrying the commensurable family $\{\log p\}$. $\mathrm{THH}/\mathrm{TC}$ over $\mathbb{S}$ is **global**: one circle action carries the whole family. The relationship is not competition but assembly: $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z}; \mathbb{Z}_p)$ and its cyclotomic Frobenius recover the prismatic/$q$-de Rham picture after $p$-completion (Bhatt-Morrow-Scholze, "Topological Hochschild homology and integral $p$-adic Hodge theory"), so Direction 3's outputs become the $p$-completed pieces of Direction 10's global object.

## 3. Formal kernel and the flow

- **Object:** $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$ as the diagonal self-intersection; $\mathrm{TC}(\mathbb{Z})$ as the Frobenius-fixed cohomology that should carry the signature.
- **Flow:** the $S^1$-action $\rho: S^1 \times \mathrm{THH} \to \mathrm{THH}$. Its universal cover gives the Deninger $\mathbb{R}$-flow $\Phi_t$; the rotation parameter is the continuous "time" carrying all $\log p$ simultaneously.
- **Per-prime recovery:** $\varphi_p$ on the $C_p$-fixed points reproduces the $(1,p)$ local bidegree of #25, but now as the restriction of one global map, not $\aleph_0$ unrelated maps.
- **Target signature:** a Hermitian/$\ast$-structure on $\pi_\ast \mathrm{TC}(\mathbb{Z})$ (or a real-analytic refinement; the archimedean place must enter, see LEARNINGS #22-24) whose definiteness on a primitive piece is RH.

## 4. Why it could break Davenport-Heilbronn (K2)

$\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$ exists because $\mathbb{Z}$ is a ring spectrum: the construction uses the actual *multiplication* of $\mathbb{Z}$, which is the Euler product's home. The Davenport-Heilbronn function is a fixed $\mathbb{C}$-linear combination of L-functions; there is no ring whose $\mathrm{THH}$ produces it. No multiplication $\Rightarrow$ no $\mathrm{THH}$ $\Rightarrow$ no surface. This is the most categorical form of the D-H discipline yet: the wrong-approach detector becomes "is there a ring spectrum here at all?"

## 5. Kill-criteria status

- **K1 (signature not trace):** plausibly clean, *if* the positivity comes from an equivariant index/signature on $\mathrm{TC}$ rather than from re-deriving the explicit formula as a Lefschetz trace. This is the crux risk: an equivariant trace formula on $\mathrm{TC}$ that just reproduces Connes' Weil distribution would be killed by R3.5. The work must locate a genuine signature.
- **K2:** clean (no ring spectrum for D-H).
- **K3 (specialize to Weil):** $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{F}_q)$ and its cyclotomic Frobenius are well understood (Bökstedt; Hesselholt-Madsen); the function-field specialization should recover the $\mathbb{F}_q$ Frobenius and the $(1,q)$ single-scale picture. Verifying this is milestone 10.3 and is the most tractable check.

## 6. First step (theory note, no quick experiment)

Write a 2K-style intersection dictionary (cf. LEARNINGS #24) with $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})/\mathrm{TC}(\mathbb{Z})$ in the "product surface" slot, and check whether the $S^1$-equivariant trace / Euler characteristic reproduces the dynamical zeta of LEARNINGS #26, $-\zeta'/\zeta(s) = \sum_n \Lambda(n) n^{-s}$. If the equivariant Euler characteristic of $\mathrm{THH}(\mathbb{Z})$ does **not** produce the von Mangoldt sum, the analogy is decorative and the direction is dropped. If it does, Direction 3 should be re-pointed toward $\mathrm{TC}$ over $\mathbb{S}$.

This decisive checkpoint is developed in full in the companion note [`10A_thh_vonmangoldt_checkpoint.md`](10A_thh_vonmangoldt_checkpoint.md): the rigorous substrate (Bökstedt, Nikolaus-Scholze, BMS), three increasingly demanding forms of the checkpoint (Euler-factor / assembled-determinant / signature), two parallel routes to attempt it, explicit kill conditions, and an effort estimate (the cheapest decisive milestone in the Directions 1-13 portfolio).

## 7. Honest assessment

This is the most likely-to-be-structurally-right of the five proposed directions, because it is the only one that *dissolves* finding #25's obstruction (one circle action carrying all primes) rather than working around it. It is also the most demanding: it requires fluency in $\mathrm{THH}/\mathrm{TC}$, cyclotomic spectra, and the Nikolaus-Scholze formalism, plus a genuinely new idea for where the *signature* (as opposed to a trace) lives. Probability of closing RH: low, but the partial results (a clean THH/TC reformulation of the Deninger flow; an equivariant computation of $\Gamma_S^2$) would be independently valuable.
